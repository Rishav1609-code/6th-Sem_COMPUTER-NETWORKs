# Computer Networks – Unit 1 Notes

# 6. Transmission Media (MAKAUT Exam-Oriented Notes)

---

# 📖 Topic Overview

Transmission Media refers to the physical path or communication channel through which data travels from a sender to a receiver.

In computer networks, transmission media acts as the bridge that carries signals between devices.

Without transmission media:

❌ No communication is possible.

❌ No Internet connection exists.

❌ No data transfer occurs.

# 6.1 Guided Transmission Media

---

## Definition

Guided Transmission Media uses a physical path such as cables to transmit data.

Signals travel through a fixed medium.

---

## Characteristics

✅ High security

✅ Less interference

✅ Reliable communication

✅ Physical connection required

---

## Types of Guided Media

```text
Guided Media
     │
 ┌───┼────────────┐
 │   │            │
Twisted Pair  Coaxial
                 │
             Optical Fiber
```

---

# 1. Twisted Pair Cable

---

## Definition

A Twisted Pair Cable consists of two insulated copper wires twisted together to reduce electromagnetic interference.

---

## Structure

```text
Wire A )))))))))) Wire B
```

The twisting reduces noise and crosstalk.

---

## Features

* Most commonly used cable
* Low cost
* Easy installation
* Flexible

---

## Applications

* Telephone Networks
* LAN Networks
* Ethernet Connections

---

# Types of Twisted Pair Cable

---

# A. UTP (Unshielded Twisted Pair)

---

## Definition

UTP does not contain any additional shielding around the twisted wires.

---

## Structure

```text
Outer Cover
    │
Twisted Wires
```

---

## Advantages

✅ Cheap

✅ Lightweight

✅ Easy installation

✅ Flexible

---

## Disadvantages

❌ More noise interference

❌ Less secure

---

## Applications

* Ethernet LAN
* Home Networks
* Office Networks

---

# B. STP (Shielded Twisted Pair)

---

## Definition

STP contains metallic shielding around twisted wires.

---

## Structure

```text
Shield
  │
Twisted Wires
```

---

## Advantages

✅ Better noise protection

✅ Less electromagnetic interference

✅ More secure

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Expensive

❌ Difficult installation

---

## Applications

* Industrial Networks
* High-Interference Areas

---

# UTP vs STP (Very Important)

| Feature          | UTP         | STP        |
| ---------------- | ----------- | ---------- |
| Shielding        | No          | Yes        |
| Cost             | Low         | High       |
| Installation     | Easy        | Difficult  |
| Noise Resistance | Low         | High       |
| Flexibility      | High        | Medium     |
| Usage            | Home/Office | Industrial |

---

# 2. Coaxial Cable

---

## Definition

A Coaxial Cable consists of a central conductor surrounded by insulation, metallic shielding, and an outer cover.

---

## Structure

```text
Outer Jacket
    │
Metal Shield
    │
Insulation
    │
Copper Core
```

---

## Features

* Better shielding than twisted pair
* Supports higher frequencies
* Longer transmission distance

---

## Advantages

✅ Less noise

✅ Better bandwidth

✅ Durable

---

## Disadvantages

❌ More expensive than twisted pair

❌ Less flexible

---

## Applications

* Cable TV
* Broadband Internet
* CCTV Systems

---

# 3. Optical Fiber Cable

---

## Definition

Optical Fiber transmits data using light signals instead of electrical signals.

---

## Structure

```text
Core
 │
Cladding
 │
Protective Jacket
```

---

## Working Principle

Uses Total Internal Reflection (TIR).

Light travels through the fiber core.

---

## Features

✅ Very high speed

✅ Long distance communication

✅ Immune to electromagnetic interference

✅ High bandwidth

---

## Advantages

✅ Extremely fast

✅ High security

✅ Low signal loss

✅ Lightweight

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Expensive

❌ Difficult installation

❌ Fragile

---

## Applications

* Internet Backbone
* Submarine Cables
* Telecommunications
* Data Centers

---

# Types of Optical Fiber

---

# A. Single Mode Fiber (SMF)

---

## Definition

Uses a single light path.

---

## Characteristics

* Small core diameter
* Long-distance communication
* High bandwidth

---

## Applications

* Telecommunication Networks
* Long-Distance Internet Links

---

# B. Multi Mode Fiber (MMF)

---

## Definition

Allows multiple light paths.

---

## Characteristics

* Larger core diameter
* Shorter distance
* Lower cost

---

## Applications

* Campus Networks
* LANs
* Data Centers

---

# Single Mode vs Multi Mode

| Feature    | Single Mode | Multi Mode |
| ---------- | ----------- | ---------- |
| Core Size  | Small       | Large      |
| Distance   | Long        | Short      |
| Cost       | High        | Lower      |
| Bandwidth  | Higher      | Lower      |
| Light Path | Single      | Multiple   |

---

# Guided Media Comparison

| Feature        | Twisted Pair | Coaxial | Optical Fiber |
| -------------- | ------------ | ------- | ------------- |
| Cost           | Low          | Medium  | High          |
| Speed          | Medium       | High    | Very High     |
| Bandwidth      | Medium       | High    | Very High     |
| Noise Immunity | Low          | Medium  | Excellent     |
| Distance       | Short        | Medium  | Long          |
| Security       | Low          | Medium  | High          |

---

# 6.2 Unguided Transmission Media

---

## Definition

Unguided Media transmits signals through air, vacuum, or space without physical cables.

---

## Characteristics

✅ Wireless communication

✅ Mobility

✅ Easy deployment

❌ More interference

❌ Lower security

---

## Classification

```text
Unguided Media
      │
 ┌────┼─────────┐
 │    │         │
Radio Microwave Infrared
                 │
            Bluetooth
                 │
               Wi-Fi
```

---

# 1. Radio Waves

---

## Definition

Radio Waves are electromagnetic waves used for wireless communication.

---

## Characteristics

* Omnidirectional
* Long-distance coverage
* Can penetrate buildings

---

## Applications

* Radio Broadcasting
* Mobile Networks
* Television Broadcasting

---

## Advantages

✅ Large coverage

✅ No alignment required

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Noise interference

❌ Security issues

---

# 2. Microwaves

---

## Definition

Microwaves are high-frequency electromagnetic waves used for point-to-point communication.

---

## Characteristics

* Line-of-sight communication
* High bandwidth
* Directional

---

# Types of Microwaves

---

# A. Terrestrial Microwave

---

## Definition

Communication between ground-based microwave towers.

---

## Structure

```text
Tower A  ─────► Tower B
```

---

## Applications

* Mobile Networks
* Long-Distance Communication

---

## Advantages

✅ High speed

✅ No cables required

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Requires line of sight

❌ Weather sensitive

---

# B. Satellite Microwave

---

## Definition

Communication through satellites.

---

## Structure

```text
Earth Station
      │
      ▼
 Satellite
      │
      ▼
Earth Station
```

---

## Applications

* GPS
* Television Broadcasting
* Global Communication

---

## Advantages

✅ Very large coverage

✅ Global connectivity

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Expensive

❌ High propagation delay

---

# Terrestrial vs Satellite Microwave

| Feature  | Terrestrial | Satellite |
| -------- | ----------- | --------- |
| Distance | Medium      | Global    |
| Cost     | Lower       | Higher    |
| Delay    | Low         | High      |
| Coverage | Regional    | Worldwide |

---

# 3. Infrared Communication

---

## Definition

Infrared communication uses infrared light waves for short-range communication.

---

## Characteristics

* Short distance
* Cannot penetrate walls
* Line-of-sight communication

---

## Applications

* TV Remote Controls
* Wireless Mouse
* Sensors

---

## Advantages

✅ Cheap

✅ Secure

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Very short range

❌ Obstacle sensitive

---

# 4. Bluetooth Communication

---

## Definition

Bluetooth is a short-range wireless technology used for device-to-device communication.

---

## Features

* Low power consumption
* Short range
* Wireless connectivity

---

## Range

Typically 10–100 meters.

---

## Applications

* Wireless Headphones
* Smart Watches
* File Sharing

---

## Advantages

✅ Low power

✅ Easy pairing

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Limited range

❌ Lower speed than Wi-Fi

---

# 5. Wi-Fi Communication

---

## Definition

Wi-Fi is a wireless networking technology based on IEEE 802.11 standards.

---

## Features

* Wireless Internet Access
* High-speed communication
* LAN connectivity

---

## Working

```text
Device
   │
Wi-Fi Router
   │
Internet
```

---

## Applications

* Home Networks
* Office Networks
* Public Hotspots

---

## Advantages

✅ High speed

✅ Mobility

✅ Easy installation

---

## Disadvantages

❌ Security risks

❌ Signal interference

---

# Bluetooth vs Wi-Fi

| Feature      | Bluetooth   | Wi-Fi           |
| ------------ | ----------- | --------------- |
| Range        | Short       | Longer          |
| Speed        | Lower       | Higher          |
| Power Usage  | Low         | Higher          |
| Applications | Accessories | Internet Access |
| Standard     | IEEE 802.15 | IEEE 802.11     |
